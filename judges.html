How to Become an ASFA Judge

Becoming an ASFA judge is a multi-step process designed to ensure all judges possess deep knowledge of the rules, safety procedures, and sighthound running styles. Below is a simplified overview.

1

Basic Eligibility

  • Must be at least 18 years old and in good standing with the ASFA.
  • Must have a general knowledge of the coursing abilities of ASFA-recognized breeds.
  • Must be familiar with the ASFA's Running Rules and Field Procedures.
  • Must have participated in lure field trials for a minimum of three (3) consecutive years.
  • Must have served in some official field capacity at a minimum of ten (10) field trials (e.g., Field Trial Chairman, Huntmaster, Secretary, Clerk, or Lure Operator).
2

Apprentice Status

  • File the Apprentice Judge License application (JDG 01) and pay the fee.
  • Successfully complete the ASFA open-book test (minimum score 85%).
  • Once granted, the apprentice name is published twice on the ASFA website.
  • As an apprentice, you must judge all courses for a breed (with at least 3 entries) under 3 different licensed judges at 3 separate trials.
  • This must be repeated for five individual breeds.

Note: You must accumulate all judging sheets and Confirmation Statements (JDG 02) to submit within one year of your last apprentice assignment.

3

Provisional Status

  • After approval, you become a Provisional Judge. You may accept assignments for all breeds, but no more than 4 provisional breeds per trial (without Regional Director permission).
  • To become fully licensed for a breed, you must provisionally judge the performance of at least five hounds in one stake OR seven hounds in one breed.
  • This must happen at three trials, under three different fully licensed judges.
  • Submit the Request for Change in Status form (JDG 03) along with completed Confirmation Statements.
4

Regular License & All-Breed

  • Once published and approved, you are a Fully Licensed Judge for those specific breeds.
  • After achieving full licensure in eight (8) breeds, a judge may apply for an All-Breed License.
  • All judges (Apprentice, Provisional, All-Breed) must retake the ASFA open-book test every four (4) years to maintain their license.
